Flowers is the third single by American singer and songwriter Oliver Tree off his upcoming fourth studio album Love You Madly, Hate You Badly. It was released on February 20, 2026.


== Background ==
Flowers precedes the releases of Love You Madly, Hate You Badly's first two singles, "Superhero" and "Joyride". Both being released in 2025, Tree did not release any new singles to support the album until he started to tease an unnamed song, initially titled by fans as "Flowers on My Tombstone", in February 2026 through social media. In that same month, Tree announced the title and release date of Flowers to be before or on Valentine's Day as a "breakup anthem". However, due to unknown delays, the single was postponed to February 20.


== Music video ==
The music video features Tree dressed as a pilot while doing various activities such as dancing whilst cleaning the cabins and outsides of aircraft. Like the music videos for Superhero and Joyride, the video was shot in Riga, Latvia.


== Composition ==
Flowers runs on for a total of two minutes and 27 seconds. According to Prelude Press, the song "blends his signature blend of pop, alternative, and experimental sounds, reflecting his ongoing mission to create music that transcends genres and connects listeners worldwide." The single falls into genres such as alternative pop and is a "melancholic yet sarcastic reflection on love, emotional neglect, and the irony of late realizations."


== Critical reception ==
Imro highlights on Tree's "fearless exploration of musical boundaries", saying Flowers, "arrives on the heels of Tree’s unprecedented global creative journey. Over the last two years, Tree recorded the upcoming album across seven continents and 80 countries, from Africa to China to Afghanistan, resulting in a record that is both adventurous and deeply personal." Applauding the music video, they praised Tree for "pairing relatable, straight-faced humor with his unmistakable off-the-wall antics – from dancing atop an airplane wing in full pilot uniform to meticulously vacuuming the cabin." In That Eric Alper, they analyze that Flowers is "a sharp left turn and a bold step forward from his upcoming fourth studio album, ‘Love You Madly, Hate You Badly,’ arriving this Spring" and "is a fully realized creative arc unfolding in real time." Emily McCormack from Melodic Magazine gave the single a review, saying "While the pissed-off lyrics may call for a much angrier tone, the Santa Cruz artist continues to combine this anger with cheery alternative-pop beats in his usual genre-bending fashion. He retrospectively explores a toxic relationship and hoping the worst for the heartbreaker: “I hate your f–cking guts, I hope you’ll always feel regret / I hope you fall and snap your neck and die a violent death / I wish we had never met, I’ll hate you ’til the day I’m dead.” In an article by Stereo Saints, they state that "“Flowers” arrives as the latest preview of his upcoming fourth studio album, Love You Madly, Hate You Badly, set for release in Spring 2026. The album was recorded across seven continents and 80 countries over the past two years — a globe-spanning creative process that feels almost mythic in scale. It's an ambitious backdrop for a track that sounds deceptively playful on the surface but cuts deep lyrically" and "rides a buoyant alt-pop groove, bright and immediate. But then the lyrics hit. The chorus includes lines like, “I hate your f–cking guts…” and “I hope you fall and snap your neck and die a violent death…” — jarringly direct sentiments wrapped in glossy production."
