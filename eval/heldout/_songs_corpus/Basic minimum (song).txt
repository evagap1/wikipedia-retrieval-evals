"Basic Minimum" (Russian: «Базовый минимум») is a single by Sabi and Mia Boyka, released in 2025. The song gained wide popularity due to its ironic lyrics about high expectations in modern relationships, becoming a viral hit on social media and streaming platform charts.


== History ==
In July 2025, Mia Boyka posted a video on her TikTok account featuring a short excerpt from the new song "Basic Minimum". Audiences responded positively to the collaboration with Sabi. The sound soon spread widely across social media. On TikTok, more than 773,000 videos were created using the excerpt, and on Instagram, more than 132,000 Reels videos used it.
One of the key factors in the song's popularity was a video by bloggers from Ulan-Ude, who filmed a humorous skit with flowers and a dance, using a bright rag instead of a wig. The clip quickly spread online and helped set the trend's comic tone.
In mid-August, the track was added to the playlist of Like FM.
By August 25, 2025, less than a month after its release, the song had surpassed 5 million streams across streaming platforms.
By mid-September 2025, "Basic Minimum" topped the VK Music chart, reaching number one. Its success beyond local platforms was also reflected in Western streaming statistics: by early 2026, the song had surpassed 10 million streams on Spotify.
In December 2025, the singers performed the song at New Song of the Year 2025.


== Critical reception ==
The professional psychology community on the B17 portal analyzed the reasons why the track provoked a strongly negative reaction among male listeners. Psychologies magazine examined the meaning of the lyrics in the context of contemporary values.
