"Bakuon" (stylized as "Bakuon -爆音-") is a song by the Japanese four-member boy group Wolf Howl Harmony. It was released digitally on March 12, 2025, as the lead track from their first album Wolf. The lyrics and composition were handled by Kenya Fujita, Chaki Zulu, and T.Kura.


== Background and release ==
The song was issued as the lead track from the group's first album, Wolf, released on March 12, 2025. Producer Chaki Zulu stated that the team “worked through trial and error to create a track that could allow Wolf Howl Harmony to take the world by storm.”


== Composition, lyrics, and musical structure ==
"Bakuon" is a dance track characterized by Chaki Zulu's intricate production style and pronounced tempo shifts. The work as a whole was created with the intention of presenting a new musical direction for the group. The track incorporates elements of baile funk (Brazilian funk), a genre not commonly adopted by existing boy groups. In an interview, member Ryoji referenced fellow member Ghee's Brazilian roots, stating that “an artist’s roots, clear axis, and message are essential.”


== Music video ==
The music video was released on March 12, 2025, the same day as the album. It was directed by Yerd, with choreography by Naoya Nagai, director of the professional dance team Lifull Alt-Rhythm. Many members of Lifull Alt-Rhythm also participated as dancers. The video was filmed inside a ruined building, featuring the four members performing choreography within a dystopian visual setting.


== Promotion and performances ==
Wolf Howl Harmony made their first appearance on the TBS music program CDTV Live! Live! on May 5, 2025, performing the song.
