"Boat" is a song by American rapper Trim, released on November 28, 2025. The track is widely regarded as her breakout single, gaining viral traction on TikTok and other video-sharing platforms, which significantly boosted its popularity. The song spawned two official remixes: one featuring Atlanta-based rappers YK Niece and Bunna B, and another featuring Memphis rapper Pooh Shiesty.


== Release and promotion ==
The single was first teased by Trim through a series of short-form videos posted across TikTok and other social media platforms. On November 24, 2025, she uploaded a video of herself lip-syncing to the track while standing in front of a large boat, simultaneously posting it to TikTok and Instagram. In the post, she announced that the song would be released on November 28, 2025. The clip quickly gained traction due to her exaggerated expressions and animated delivery, drawing widespread comparisons to the early career persona of Nicki Minaj and contributing to the song's viral spread across video-sharing platforms.
On December 23, 2025, Trim released an official remix of featuring rappers YK Niece and Bunna B, accompanied by a music video. The remix served as the song's first major expansion and further increased its visibility online. A second remix featuring Memphis rapper Pooh Shiesty was released on March 20, 2026, alongside an additional music video, continuing the track's promotional run and introducing it to a broader audience.


== Composition ==
Musically, "Boat" is a bass-heavy hip hop track built around a minimal, looping instrumental that emphasizes rhythm and vocal delivery over complex structure. The production has been described as sparse and hypnotic, allowing Trim's cadence and tone to take center stage.
Trim's performance on the song is characterized by an animated, expressive delivery, incorporating exaggerated inflections and playful vocal shifts. Her use of tone and cadence has drawn widespread comparisons to Nicki Minaj, particularly her early "Roman" era, with some critics noting similarities in both vocal styling and persona. According to AllHipHop, the track's "sharp delivery" and "expressive mannerisms" sparked immediate comparisons and online debate surrounding influence and originality.


== TikTok virality ==
"Boat" experienced significant viral success on TikTok shortly after its release, driven largely by Trim's own promotional videos and her animated delivery, which sparked widespread comparisons to early Nicki Minaj. The original teaser clip—featuring Trim lip-syncing with exaggerated expressions—quickly circulated across the platform, leading to a surge in user-generated content and helping establish the track as a trending sound.
The song was further amplified by celebrity engagement, with artists such as South African singer Tyla and rapper Rico Nasty, among those who used the audio in their own TikTok posts, contributing to its visibility and cultural reach. According to coverage from The Fader, the track garnered widespread attention on the platform, accumulating millions of likes and interactions while attracting co-signs from multiple artists.
By April 2026, "Boat" had been used in over 650,000 TikTok posts across its original and remixed versions, reflecting its sustained popularity on the platform. The song also entered the top 50 of TikTok's most popular tracks in the United States, highlighting its strong performance in user engagement and content creation.


== Music video ==
The official music video for the remix of "Boat" featuring YK Niece and Bunna B was released on December 23, 2025, the same day as the single. The video accompanied the track's initial remix rollout and accumulated over 1.6 million views on YouTube as of April 2026.
A second music video, supporting the remix featuring Pooh Shiesty, was released on March 20, 2026, coinciding with the single's release. The video saw rapid engagement upon release, surpassing 1 million views on YouTube within its first week.
