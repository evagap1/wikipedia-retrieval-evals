Schizonycha effeta is a species of beetle of the family Scarabaeidae. It is found in South Africa (Limpopo, Eastern Cape).


== Description ==
Adults reach a length of about 10–11 mm (0.39–0.43 in). The head and pronotum are testaceous-red, while the elytra are paler testaceous. The surface of both the clypeus and frontal part of the head is covered with large, round, nearly coalescing punctures separated by a very narrow, slightly raised wall. The pronotum is covered with cicatricose punctures divided by a smooth space equal to their own diameter, and slightly larger and less cicatricose in the centre, where there is a very small impunctate area. The supra-basal fold is somewhat punctate. The scutellum has one lateral row of punctures and the elytra are covered with sub-cicatricose punctures separated by an interval equal to their diameter and almost imperceptibly setigerous. The pygidium has a few punctures only on the sides of the anterior part.
