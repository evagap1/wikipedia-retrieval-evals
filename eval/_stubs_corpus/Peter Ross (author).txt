Peter Ross is a Scottish journalist and non-fiction author based in Glasgow. He is a nine-times winner at the Scottish Press Awards  and was a finalist for the Orwell Prize for Journalism in 2015.
Ross is known for his observational and narrative non-fiction exploring Scottish life, landscapes, graveyards, churches, and ancient sites across Britain and Ireland. His work has appeared in publications including The Guardian, The Times, National Geographic Traveler, Smithsonian Magazine and Boston Review.
His book A Tomb With a View won the Saltire Society Scottish Non-Fiction Book of the Year in 2021. A Tomb With a View and Upon a White Horse have both been adapted as BBC Radio 4 Book of the Week.


== Books ==
Daunderlust: Dispatches from Unreported Scotland (2014) — A collection of essays on Scottish life.
The Passion of Harry Bingo: Further Dispatches from Unreported Scotland (2017) — Shortlisted for the Saltire Society Scottish Non-Fiction Book of the Year.
A Tomb With a View: The Stories & Glories of Graveyards (2020) — Explores graveyards across Britain and Ireland. Won the Saltire Society Scottish Non-Fiction Book of the Year (2021) and was named a book of the year by the Financial Times, i, and Stylist.
Steeple Chasing: Around Britain by Church (2023) — A journey through Britain's churches and cathedrals. A Sunday Times bestseller and Waterstones Non-Fiction Book of the Month.
Upon a White Horse: Journeys in Ancient Britain and Ireland (2025) — Explores prehistoric sites and ancient landscapes. A Radio 4 Book of the Week and named a best book of 2025 by the Financial Times, The Scotsman, and Country Life.
