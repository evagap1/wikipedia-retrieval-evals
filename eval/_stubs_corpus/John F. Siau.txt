John Finn Siau (1921–1996) was an American wood scientist and researcher known for his contributions to the study of moisture relations, permeability, and transport processes in wood.
He was an elected fellow of the International Academy of Wood Science, and had been associated with the Department of Wood Science and Forest Products at Virginia Polytechnic Institute, and the SUNY College of Environmental Science and Forestry in Syracuse.


== Career and research ==
He received his PhD in 1968 from the State University College of Forestry at Syracuse University, doing a dissertation titled, “The physical properties of wood-polymer composites”.
Siau carried out most of his research on the physical properties of wood, particularly the role of moisture, transport phenomena, and wood structure. His work has contributed to the understanding of water movement, diffusion, and permeability in wood materials, which are critical for wood drying, preservation, and utilization.
He has also supervised graduate students in wood science and related fields, including research in non-isothermal moisture diffusion in wood. These works address topics such as wood anatomy, permeability, capillarity, thermodynamics of sorption, and moisture diffusion. His models (e.g., non-isothermal diffusion) are widely cited in wood drying and transport research.
His book entitled, "Transport Processes in Wood" has been cited more than 2,000 times globally, and until April 2026, his overall research work has received more than 4,700 citations at Google Scholar.
