"Amay Ektu Jayga Dao" (Bengali: আমায় একটু জায়গা দাও মায়ের মন্দিরে বসি) is a Bengali language song by Indian playback singer Manna Dey, originally written by Pulak Bandyopadhyay and composed by Mrinal Bandyopadhyay. It is also known by the title "Amay Ektu Jayga Dao Mayer Mondire Bosi", from the album Maa Amar Maa.
The song was one of the last songs written by Pulak Bandyopadhyay, before he committed suicide by jumping into the Hooghly River and ended his musical career. "Amay Ektu Jayga Dao" is considered to be the last historical hit of Manna Dey.


== Overview ==
The song, after being written by Pulak Bandyopadhyay in 1998, was given to the composer Abhijit Banerjee to compose its music. Banerjee was not able to complete its while composition till the end of the song. Pulak Bandyopadhyay was not satisfied with the music and remarked it as "not coming alive." The song was then given to Mrinal Bandyopadhyay for composition, which was approved by Pulak Bandyopadhyay. 
Mrinal Bandyopadhyay was given the task to compose the first two songs of the album Maa Amar Maa, which were "Amay Ektu Jayga Dao" and "Jakhan Emon Hoy Jibonta Mone Hoy Byartho Aborjona." After he had composed the songs, Mrinal Bandyopadhyay (Banerjee) sang the two songs in Pulak Bandyopadhyay's house while his eyes were closed. Finishing singing the songs, he did not find Bandyopadhyay in the house when he opened his eyes. Mrinal Banerjee thought that he had sung the songs to himself while no one else was in the house. He thought if Bandyopadhyay had not liked the songs, and was sad. Banerjee went to find if Bandyopadhyay was there in the house. He was surprised to find that Bandyopadhyay was standing in the balcony with his back turned towards the door. He saw Pulak Bandyopadhyay crying upon calling him. Bandyopadhyay remarked, "What a music you gave Mrinal! I wanted music just like this." 
The music video of the song aired on the television channel Doordarshan during the years 1998 to 1999, by the Gathani Company. It showed Manna Dey singing the song while sitting on the terrace of the Dakhineswar Kali Temple. The music video was a factor for making the song successful. It was shown several times per day. After Manna Dey died, a cover version of "Amay Ektu Jayga Dao" was made many times, such as by his nephew Sudeb Dey and Indian singer Kumar Sanu. 
Abhijit Banerjee, who the song was first composed by, commented " 'Amay Ektu Jayga Dao' was first composed by me. I left it until the end. After listening to the songs by Manna Dey, I realized that I had composed the song while thinking about Manna Dey. But Mrinal composed the song thinking about 'Maa', that's why it became a milestone.
