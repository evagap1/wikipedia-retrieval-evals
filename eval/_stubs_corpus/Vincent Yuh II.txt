Chief Vincent Yuh II was a Cameroonian traditional ruler who served as the Fon of Kom from 1994 until his death in 2017. He was the fourteenth ruler of the Kom kingdom in the Northwest Region of Cameroon.


== Early life ==
Before his enthronement, Vincent Yuh II was a trader and served as a village head in Anjin, one of the villages within the Kom fondom.


== Reign (1994–2017) ==
Vincent Yuh II was crowned Fon of Kom on 17 September 1994, succeeding Fon Yibain.
During his reign, he was regarded as a promoter of peace and development within the Kom kingdom. He presided over traditional institutions and played a central role in maintaining cultural and administrative authority in the fondom.
The Kom kingdom, headquartered in Laikom, is one of the major traditional polities in Cameroon’s Northwest Region, where customary institutions remain influential in governance and social organization.


== Death and succession ==
Fon Vincent Yuh II died on 22 November 2017 at the age of 97 after a prolonged illness at the Laikom Palace.
He was succeeded by Fon Clement Ndzi II, who became the fifteenth Fon of Kom following traditional succession rites.


== Legacy ==
Vincent Yuh II is remembered as a long-reigning monarch who contributed to stability and development within the Kom fondom. His leadership reinforced the role of traditional authority in community governance and cultural preservation.
