Baforchu (also known as Mbu or Bafochu) is a village and traditional chiefdom located in the Santa Subdivision of Mezam Division in the Northwest Region of Cameroon.


== Overview ==
Baforchu is part of the Grassfields cultural region and belongs to the Ngemba-speaking communities of Cameroon. Baforchu is home to a variety of colanuts that grow in its hilly topography, a key characteristic of the village.


== Population ==
According to the 2005 national census, Baforchu had a population of approximately 884 inhabitants.


== Culture ==
Languages spoken include Ngamambo and Moghamo, both part of the Grassfields Bantu language group.
