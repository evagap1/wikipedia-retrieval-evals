Clann Lir (from Irish - "Children of Lir) is a Russian folk group-turned-supergroup performing ancient Celtic songs: Irish, Scottish, and Welsh.   
The ensemble Clann Lir is a side project of the leaders of famous Moscow folk groups: singer-songwriter Hellawes from Melnitsa, bagpiper Vladimir Lazerson from Tintal, singer Yuri Andreychuk from Voinstvo Sidov, and composer and keyboardist Konstantin Vyshinsky.
Unlike the main bands of the members, Clann Lir performs not their own compositions in the folk music or folk rock styles, but rather traditional Celtic folk songs. The group's repertoire consists of songs in the languages ​​of the Celtic peoples  (Irish, Gaelic, and Welsh) and secular Irish music from the 18th century.
The band's name translates from Irish as "Children of Lir" or "Family of Lir" and refers to the ancient Irish legend of the Children of Lir.


== History ==

The band was formed in 2003 by experienced musicians from the Moscow Celtic scene. The original lineup included Natalia O'Shea, Vladimir Lazerson, Yuri Andreychuk, and Konstantin Vyshinsky.
The group began performing in clubs in Moscow and St. Petersburg.
By the time the band was formed, the members of Clann Lir had achieved some success and fame as members of several folk-rock bands: Melnitsa, Tintal, and Slua Si (The Sidhe Host). Thus, their side project Clann Lir is a supergroup.
The band's debut album, entitled Clann Lir , was released in 2005. The album's concert presentation took place on March 20, 2005, at the Veresk club in Moscow as part of the four-day Celts Non-Stop festival, timed to coincide with St. Patrick's Day.
In 2006, harpist Tatyana Strunina joined the group. The album "Clann Lir" was reissued in 2008 by Perekrestok Records.
