Mekanya Charles Okon is a Cameroonian agro-industrial executive and traditional leader who served as Managing Director of Pamol Plantations Plc, a state-owned palm oil company in Cameroon’s South-West Region. He is also associated with traditional leadership structures within the Oroko area of Ndian Division.


== Early life and traditional status ==
Mekanya Charles Okon is a traditional chief from the Oroko cultural area in Ndian Division, South-West Region of Cameroon. He is listed among chiefs of Oroko clans, specifically associated with the Bera Bakoko lineage.


== Career ==


=== Early career and PAMOL leadership ===
Preceding his appointment as Managing Director, Charles held senior roles within Cameroon’s agro-industrial sector. By 2011, he was identified as Deputy General Manager of Pamol Plantations Plc.
Following the death of then Managing Director Obi-Okpun Wanobi Osang on 28 December 2012, Charles assumed leadership of Pamol in an interim capacity from 2013.


=== Managing Director of Pamol Plantations Plc (2016–2020) ===
Charles was officially confirmed as Managing Director of Pamol Plantations Plc on 10 July 2016.
Pamol Plantations Plc is a public agro-industrial enterprise specializing in oil palm cultivation and processing in Cameroon’s South-West Region, managing over 11,000 hectares and employing approximately 2,500 workers at the time of his appointment.
During his term, Charles oversaw strategic initiatives including:

Expansion of plantations into the Bakassi Peninsula, aimed at economic development and territorial consolidation.
Support for smallholder schemes and increased processing capacity at company mills.
He highlighted challenges affecting the company, including fluctuations in global palm oil prices and structural limitations in marketing channels.


=== Later roles and departure ===
Charles remained at the helm of Pamol until October 2020, when he was replaced as Managing Director during a board restructuring.
He has also been associated with administrative and financial roles in regional development structures, including serving as Director of Administration and Finance at SOWEDA (South West Development Authority) as of 2020.


== Political and public engagements ==
Charles has been involved in local political and administrative structures, including participation in electoral coordination bodies in Ndian Division under the Cameroon People’s Democratic Movement (CPDM).


== Controversies ==
In 2015, allegations published by a local newspaper concerning corruption at Pamol and implicating Charles were ruled by Cameroon’s National Communication Council as unsubstantiated and in violation of journalistic ethics, leading to sanctions against the publication. This sometimes is cited in discussions of media-state relations rather than as evidence of managerial malpractice.


== Historiography and academic analysis ==
Academic and policy-oriented interpretations of Mekanya Charles Charles’s career situate his leadership within broader debates on state-owned agro-industrial enterprises in Cameroon and Central Africa. Although no specific academic study is devoted exclusively to Charles, his term of office at Pamol Plantations Plc is frequently interpreted through three overlapping analytical frameworks: state capitalism, post-conflict territorial consolidation, and hybrid governance.
Charles’s management is often read in the context of state capitalism in Cameroon, where public agro-industrial corporations function as instruments of both economic production and political patronage. His progression from Deputy General Manager to Managing Director aligns with patterns identified in administrative studies of Cameroonian parastatals, where technocratic expertise is combined with political and regional integration.
